Research Field Experience!

Fish and Environmental Sampling


Hands-on field research has shaped my commitment to high quality data collection, safety, and teamwork. Whether in remote environments, or in front of an audience at outreach events, I have developed my field skills through a willingness to learn and a drive to excel at what I do.
These field experiences directly inform my data analysis approach, as I consider each data point, how it was obtained, and what that means for the apprporiate statistical analysis.

Fish ID and Dissections

I quickly developed the ability to identify fish species in Midwestern streams and lakes, as well as learned a variety of tropical fishes and corals while studying in Turks and Caicos. This skill was honed in dynamic field conditions, where accuracy and speed were essential. In addition to species identification, I gaiend a solid understanding of fish physiological and biology.

I performed dissections on Centrarchidae species to get morphometrics, as well as collect tissues samples for stable isotope analysis, remove otoliths and complete stomach content analysis.

SCUBA/Snorkel Surveys

Collaborated with team members to safely conduct transect surveys for tropical fish species, and participated in manta tow surveys to assess Queen Conch (Aliger gigas) populations around South Caicos. These experiences emphasized precision in data collection, situational awareness, and strong underwater communication skills.

Electrofishing

Electrofishing was one of the primary ways of sampling fish for the Wisconsin Department of Natural Resources Trout surveys and Water Quality surveys I worked on. I have experience with backpack and boat/streamshocker-mounted systems.

Boat Safety

Operated offboard motor boat. Trailored and Loaded boat. Safely maneuvered around rivers and lakes.

Live Animal Sampling

Handling live animals with care and respect is a core value of mine. I adhere closely to IACUC protocols to ensure ethical treatment at all times. When working with large or heavy fish, I took the necessary precautions to manage them safely and effectively. I strive to work calmly and efficiently in order to minimize stress on the animals during handling and data collection. When working with sensitive fish species, I consistently followed proper recovery protocols to ensure their well-being post-handling. Careful attention was given to minimizing stress and promoting full recovery before release.

Live conch were carefully collected from the ocean and temporarily housed to allow for the collection of fecal matter. After sample collection, each conch was safely returned to their habitat, and properly burped according to their physiology. Ensuring the well-being of the animals was a top priority, and all individuals were handled with care and respect throughout the process.

Sample Collection and Environmental Surveys

Eckman dredge, YSI, DO meter,Flow meter, plankton nets

Additional Sampling Method Experiences

Traps, fykes and mini fykes, seines, gill nets.

In the Field

Teamwork, safety, organization, and a positive attitude.
